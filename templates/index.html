ALARMING FACTS


The planet's average surface temperature has risen about 1.2 degrees Celsius (2.2 degrees Fahrenheit) since the late 19th century, largely due to increased carbon dioxide and other human-made emissions into the atmosphere .


Increased frequency and severity of extreme weather events such as hurricanes, heatwaves, wildfires, and heavy precipitation. For instance, the number of extreme weather events has quadrupled since 1980 .


Oceans absorb about 30% of the carbon dioxide released into the atmosphere. This has led to a 30% increase in ocean acidity since the Industrial Revolution, affecting marine life and ecosystems

Many species are at risk of extinction due to changing climate conditions. Habitats like coral reefs and rainforests, which host diverse life forms, are particularly vulnerable
